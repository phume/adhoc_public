## 1. DTOs (International) — 1,550 / 4,600 / 8,200

These are people involved in **international cocaine/heroin transit through DR** (not domestic street dealing).

**Three sub-tiers summed:**

| Sub-tier | Low | Mid | High | How we got there |
|----------|-----|-----|------|------------------|
| Leadership/principals | 50 | 100 | 200 | Anchored on Treasury Kingpin Act designees (Peralta network alone was 20+ individuals; Calderon Rijo network another 10+). At any given time, ~50-200 principals run major DR trafficking operations. |
| Mid-level transporters/operatives | 1,000 | 3,000 | 5,000 | **The anchor:** ~120 tons of cocaine transit Hispaniola annually (DNCD estimate via Vice Admiral Pimental). Go-fast boats carry ~500kg-1 ton per trip → ~120-240 major shipments/year. Each shipment needs crews (3-8 people per boat), port workers, warehouse operators, logistics. Plus air and land routes. 3,000 mid-level operatives across all routes is the mid-point. |
| Facilitators (corrupt officials, lawyers, brokers) | 500 | 1,500 | 3,000 | Global Organized Crime Index rates DR state-embedded criminality at 6.0/10. For every mid-level operative, you need ~0.5 facilitators (lawyers, customs brokers, port officials, money changers). 3,000 operatives × 0.5 = 1,500. |

**Sum:** 50+1,000+500 = **1,550** (low) ... 100+3,000+1,500 = **4,600** (mid) ... 200+5,000+3,000 = **8,200** (high)

**Key defense point:** The 120 tons/year figure is the most important anchor. 37.7 tons were seized in 2024 (DNCD record), implying a ~25-31% interdiction rate, which back-calculates to ~120-150 tons total. This is consistent with UNODC and INCSR estimates.

## 2. Street Dealers (Domestic) — 15,000 / 45,000 / 90,000

These are **domestic-market drug sellers** in the DR — selling marijuana, crack, ecstasy, etc. locally.

| Sub-tier | Low | Mid | High | How we got there |
|----------|-----|-----|------|------------------|
| Full-time dealers | 5,000 | 15,000 | 30,000 | Every major DR city has hundreds of known "puntos" (selling points). Santo Domingo alone has an estimated 1,000+ active puntos. Each punto has 2-5 full-time sellers. Across all urban areas: 5,000-30,000 is the range. |
| Part-time/occasional sellers | 10,000 | 30,000 | 60,000 | Harvard ReVista research documents drug selling as a "part-time job" in the informal economy. In a country with 54.7% informal employment, the part-time seller pool is large. Ratio of ~2:1 part-time to full-time is a standard assumption in drug market economics. |

**Sum:** 5,000+10,000 = **15,000** (low) ... 15,000+30,000 = **45,000** (mid) ... 30,000+60,000 = **90,000** (high)

**Key defense point:** This is the largest category by far, but the **least relevant for AML detection**. Most street dealers operate entirely in cash within the informal economy. Their banking penetration is estimated at only 15-30% (below the national 65% average), and their transactions are too small to trigger monitoring. This category is included for completeness but does not drive the STR estimate.

## 3. Fentanyl/Heroin Network (US-DR) — 2,700 / 7,000 / 14,000

This is the **Dominican-run fentanyl/heroin distribution network operating primarily in the US Northeast**, with financial ties back to DR.

| Sub-tier | Low | Mid | High | How we got there |
|----------|-----|-----|------|------------------|
| US-based mid-level+ distributors | 500 | 1,500 | 3,000 | **The anchor:** DEA characterizes Dominican TCOs as the "dominant retail distributors" of fentanyl/heroin in the US Northeast (NJ, NY, MA, CT, PA, RI). 6+ major DEA prosecutions of Dominican fentanyl networks in 2024-2025, each involving 10-30 defendants. Extrapolating from known cases to total network size: ~500-3,000 mid-level distributors. |
| US-based street-level dealers | 2,000 | 5,000 | 10,000 | DOJ HIDTA (High Intensity Drug Trafficking Area) assessments for NE US identify Dominican-run organizations as controlling hundreds of retail distribution points. Each mid-level distributor manages ~3-5 street dealers. 1,500 × 3-5 = 4,500-7,500, rounded to 5,000 mid. |
| DR-based supply/money operatives | 200 | 500 | 1,000 | Source-side logistics in DR: people managing money flows back to DR, coordinating supply chains, managing front businesses. Smaller than the US-side network. ~200-1,000. |

**Sum:** 500+2,000+200 = **2,700** (low) ... 1,500+5,000+500 = **7,000** (mid) ... 3,000+10,000+1,000 = **14,000** (high)

**Why 2,700 specifically for the low estimate:**
- 500 mid-level US distributors (conservative: only ~500 individuals running Dominican distribution cells across all NE states)
- 2,000 US street dealers (conservative: ~4 dealers per mid-level distributor)
- 200 DR-based operatives (conservative: source-side is smaller than distribution side)
- Total: 500 + 2,000 + 200 = 2,700

**Key defense point:** Most of these people are **in the US**, not DR. The relevance to Scotiabank DR is the **financial tail** — remittances sent from US NE back to DR, family accounts, real estate purchases, front business accounts. Only the DR-based operatives (200-1,000) directly bank in DR; the rest appear via incoming remittance patterns.

## 4. Money Mules — 900 / 3,300 / 8,600

People whose **primary role** is moving money for criminal networks through the banking system.

| Sub-tier | Low | Mid | High | How we got there |
|----------|-----|-----|------|------------------|
| US-side mules (Dominican nationals) | 500 | 2,000 | 5,000 | Multiple mule cells operate across NJ/NY/FL. Each DTO/fentanyl network maintains its own mule network. Typical cell = 5-20 mules. Dozens of cells active = 500-5,000. |
| DR-side mules (receiving/distributing) | 300 | 1,000 | 3,000 | Domestic cash distribution through cambios (exchange houses), real estate purchases, front businesses. The receiving end of the US-DR pipeline. |
| Complicit remittance/exchange staff | 100 | 300 | 600 | FinCEN specifically imposed Geographic Targeting Orders on DR-bound remittances, indicating systemic complicity among some remittance agents. 100-600 individuals in the exchange/remittance industry who knowingly facilitate ML. |

**Sum:** 500+300+100 = **900** (low) ... 2,000+1,000+300 = **3,300** (mid) ... 5,000+3,000+600 = **8,600** (high)

**Key defense point:** Money mules are **the most operationally relevant category for AML detection**. Their banking penetration is ~95-100% (bank accounts are literally their tool), and their transaction patterns are highly anomalous (rapid in/out flows, multiple remittances, structuring). The DR-side mules (300-3,000) are the prime candidates for detection at Scotiabank.

## 5. Human Trafficking — 400 / 1,100 / 2,100

| Sub-tier | Low | Mid | High | How we got there |
|----------|-----|-----|------|------------------|
| Network operators | 100 | 300 | 600 | **Anchor:** DR TIP Report 2023 reports 229 investigations opened that year. Not all investigations correspond to unique operators, but this gives a floor. At any given time, ~100-600 active operators. |
| Facilitators (recruiters, transporters, document forgers) | 300 | 800 | 1,500 | Each trafficking operation requires multiple facilitators — recruiters in source communities, transporters, people who produce false documents, people who manage safe houses. Ratio of ~2-3 facilitators per operator. |

**Sum:** 100+300 = **400** (low) ... 300+800 = **1,100** (mid) ... 600+1,500 = **2,100** (high)

**Key defense point:** DR is Tier 2 on the US TIP Report (2025). 40 people prosecuted, 20 convicted for trafficking in 2023. 100,000+ stateless Dominicans of Haitian descent are especially vulnerable. The financial footprint is primarily cash businesses + cross-border flows.

## 6. ML Professionals — 200 / 500 / 1,000

Lawyers, accountants, real estate agents, and notaries who **knowingly facilitate money laundering**.

| How we got there |
|------------------|
| **Anchor:** FATF MER 2018 rated DR's DNFBP (Designated Non-Financial Business and Professional) supervision as "Low" effectiveness. Real estate is identified by INCSR as the primary laundering vehicle in DR. There are thousands of lawyers and real estate agents in DR; the question is what fraction knowingly facilitates ML. |
| **Low (200):** Only the most egregious — lawyers/accountants directly embedded in DTO networks. |
| **Mid (500):** Includes "willful blindness" professionals who handle suspicious transactions without asking questions. |
| **High (1,000):** Includes semi-complicit professionals who benefit from ML fees without being part of organized crime. |

**Key defense point:** This category has **near-100% banking penetration** and generates detectable patterns (unusual transaction sizes for their stated profession, multiple client accounts, real estate transaction volumes). Very high priority for AML monitoring.

## 7. Corrupt Officials / PEPs — 400 / 1,100 / 2,000

| Sub-tier | Low | Mid | High | How we got there |
|----------|-----|-----|------|------------------|
| PEPs facilitating organized crime | 100 | 300 | 500 | OC Index rates DR military/police participation in trafficking as significant. Documented cases of high-ranking officials (OFAC-designated Bautista laundered $100M+). At any time, ~100-500 PEPs actively facilitating OC. |
| Corrupt law enforcement | 200 | 500 | 1,000 | Documented police purges in DR. The DEA office in Santo Domingo was itself compromised (agents corrupt). Police earn ~$300-500/month, making bribery economically rational. |
| Corrupt administrative officials (customs, ports) | 100 | 300 | 500 | Cocaine transits through DR ports and airports. This requires customs/port officials who look the other way. Each port needs ~10-20 complicit officials to enable regular shipments. |

**Sum:** 100+200+100 = **400** (low) ... 300+500+300 = **1,100** (mid) ... 500+1,000+500 = **2,000** (high)

**Why 2,000 specifically for the high estimate:**
- 500 PEPs facilitating organized crime (upper bound for senior officials across all government branches)
- 1,000 corrupt law enforcement (upper bound across DNCD, national police, military)
- 500 corrupt administrative officials (upper bound across all ports, airports, customs posts)
- Total: 500 + 1,000 + 500 = 2,000

**Key defense point:** PEPs bank formally by definition — they receive government salaries through bank accounts. The detection signal is **lifestyle inconsistency**: spending patterns (luxury, tourist zones, foreign travel) that are incompatible with their declared government salary. Transparency International CPI for DR is 37/100 (high corruption).

## 9. The Deduplication — "~30%" and What It Means

### What is deduplication?

The 7 categories are **not mutually exclusive**. The same person can appear in multiple categories. Examples:

- A **corrupt customs official** (counted in PEPs) who also **facilitates cocaine transit** (counted in DTOs) = double-counted
- A **money mule** (counted in mules) who also **distributes fentanyl** in the US (counted in fentanyl network) = double-counted
- A **lawyer** who launders money (counted in ML professionals) for a **DTO** (counted in DTOs) = double-counted
- A **street dealer** (counted in street dealers) who also acts as a **low-level mule** (counted in mules) = double-counted
- A **PEP** who runs a **nightclub front** for DTO proceeds (counted in PEPs AND DTOs) = double-counted

### Why ~30%?

The 30% deduplication factor means: **~30% of the raw sum represents double-counting, so we multiply by 0.70 to get unique individuals.**

| | Before dedup | × 0.70 | After dedup |
|---|---|---|---|
| Low | 21,150 | × 0.70 | ~14,805 → ~15,000 |
| Mid | 62,600 | × 0.70 | ~43,820 → ~44,000 |
| High | 125,900 | × 0.70 | ~88,130 → ~88,000 |

### Why 30% and not 10% or 50%?

**The reasoning:**

- **Street dealers (the biggest category)** have minimal overlap with other categories. A domestic marijuana seller is unlikely to also be a DTO operative or a PEP. This pushes the dedup factor **down** (less overlap).
- **DTOs, mules, fentanyl, and ML professionals** have significant overlap with each other. A DTO operation includes its own mules, its own lawyers, its own corrupt officials. This pushes the dedup factor **up** (more overlap).
- **PEPs and facilitators** overlap heavily with DTOs — they are counted both as "corrupt official" and as "DTO facilitator."

**Rough decomposition of the 30%:**
- ~5% overlap between DTOs and PEPs/facilitators (same people counted in both)
- ~5% overlap between DTOs and mules (DTO's own mule networks)
- ~5% overlap between fentanyl network and mules (fentanyl operation's own mule networks)
- ~5% overlap between ML professionals and DTOs (embedded lawyers/accountants)
- ~5% overlap between street dealers and mules (some street dealers also move money)
- ~5% other miscellaneous overlaps

**Net: ~30% overlap is a moderate, defensible estimate.** If challenged:
- "It could be as low as 20%" → leads to ~50,000 mid-estimate (higher)
- "It could be as high as 40%" → leads to ~37,500 mid-estimate (lower)
- The sensitivity range is ~37,500 to ~50,000 for the mid-estimate, which doesn't dramatically change downstream conclusions

### What it is NOT

- It is **not** a precision discount (we're not saying 30% of our estimates are wrong)
- It is **not** a banking penetration adjustment (that comes later in Section 4)
- It is purely an **overlap correction** for the fact that criminal roles are not mutually exclusive

## 10. The Final Line — ~15,000 / ~44,000 / ~88,000 unique illicit actors in DR

### As % of adult population (8.4M): 0.18% / 0.52% / 1.05%

**Sanity check:**
- Low (0.18%): ~1 in 560 DR adults is involved in illicit activity → feels conservative for a major drug transit country
- Mid (0.52%): ~1 in 192 DR adults → plausible for a country where INCSR says ML is "pervasive"
- High (1.05%): ~1 in 95 DR adults → upper bound, includes many part-time street dealers

**Comparison:** The UNODC estimates that globally, ~0.5-1% of GDP is seized criminal proceeds, and only ~1% of total criminal proceeds are intercepted. This implies criminal activity is far more widespread than enforcement data suggests. Our 0.5% mid-estimate for a high-risk jurisdiction is defensible.

## Quick-Reference: Anticipated Tough Questions

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| "Why is fentanyl 2,700 on the low end?" | 500 US mid-level distributors + 2,000 US street dealers + 200 DR-based operatives. Conservative count anchored on DEA prosecution data extrapolated to total network. |
| "Why is PEP/corrupt officials 2,000 on the high end?" | 500 senior PEPs + 1,000 corrupt police/military + 500 customs/port officials. Upper bound anchored on OC Index state-embedded criminality score and documented police purges. |
| "What does 'deduplicated' mean?" | Same person can appear in multiple categories (e.g., a corrupt official who facilitates DTO trafficking). We remove ~30% to avoid double-counting. |
| "Why 30% and not more/less?" | Street dealers (biggest group) have low overlap with other categories. DTOs/mules/PEPs/ML pros overlap significantly with each other. 30% is a moderate compromise. Sensitivity: 20-40% range gives ~37K-50K mid. |
| "Why are street dealers so large?" | 54.7% informal employment + hundreds of known "puntos" per major city + Harvard ReVista research on part-time drug selling. But they are the LEAST relevant for banking AML — most don't bank formally. |
| "Are these people all in DR?" | No. The fentanyl network (2,700-14,000) is **mostly in the US**. Only their financial footprint hits DR banks (remittances, family accounts). US-side mules are also primarily in the US. |
| "How confident are you in these numbers?" | These are Fermi estimates — order-of-magnitude approximations. The value is in the structure and relative sizing, not the precise numbers. The mid-estimate could easily be 30K or 60K instead of 44K. |
| "What's the most uncertain category?" | Street dealers (range is 6x from low to high) and money mules (range is ~10x). Fentanyl is also uncertain because most data comes from US-side prosecutions. |
